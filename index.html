I. The Global Water Crisis

Water scarcity affects more than 40% of the global population and is projected to rise with climate change. While 70% of the planet is covered in water, the distribution of usable freshwater is dangerously uneven.

2.2B People lack access to safely managed drinking water services worldwide.
4.2B People lack safely managed sanitation, leading to the spread of preventable diseases.
80% Wastewater resulting from human activities is discharged into rivers or sea without any pollution removal.

II. Virtual Water: The Hidden Consumption

III. Local Focus: Hong Kong's Infrastructure

Hong Kong faces unique challenges. With limited natural lakes or rivers, our survival depends on engineering and cross-border cooperation.

The Dongjiang Link

70-80% of our water travels over 70km from Guangdong. This makes us vulnerable to regional droughts.

Seawater Flushing

A global innovation. HK is one of the only cities using a separate seawater system for toilets, saving 20% of freshwater.

Desalination 2.0

The Tseung Kwan O plant uses Reverse Osmosis to provide a secure "third" water source for the city.
